THE COMPANIES

This project considers the 50 corporations in the United States with the highest revenue of the 2018 fiscal year, - these are the top 10% of the "Fortune 500." These companies collectively represent 30% of the 2018 GDP, or Gross Domestic Product, which is often used as a measure for the economy. That’s over 6 trillion dollars ($6,000,000,000,000) in revenue from only 50 companies.

These corporations are powerful players in the economy and society at large. They control the flow of materials and goods, including food and necessities. They employ millions of workers, and are responsible for prolific advertising. The fact that these companies have an impact on the environment, their employees, and the surrounding communities is undeniable.

CSR SCORES

This infographic charts the “CSR Scores” for these 50 companies, obtained from CSRHub.com. CSR stands for “Corporate Social Responsibility.”

Put simply, CSR is a measurement of a company’s influence on all aspects of society, whether good or bad. CSRHub takes into account four categories when determining CSR scores: environment, community, employees, and governance. For example, does the company cause pollution, underpay its workers, and allow for corrupt leadership on its board? Or does it make a positive impact on society instead?
